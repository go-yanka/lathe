# Workflow Reference — Direct (Fable) vs Through-the-Harness: the contest

*Two accounts of the same 21 workflows, authored two ways, then compared honestly.*
- **Direct — `WORKFLOW_REFERENCE.md`:** Fable writing freely from the source, one pass.
- **Through the harness — `WORKFLOW_REFERENCE_HARNESS.md`:** generated **100% through the live harness
  analyst path** — `request_spec` → the `claude_proxy.py` OpenAI-shim on `:8787` → `claude -p` (opus).
  Three real analyst calls, the harness's own **generate → CE-review → fold-back** loop:
  (1) the analyst **drafts** the doc, doctrine-primed, forced to ground every claim at `file:line`;
  (2) a **CE review** pass runs three lenses (correctness / docs / adversarial) over that draft and returns
  findings; (3) the analyst **folds** the findings and emits the final + a §7 recording what was caught.
  No hand-authoring — every word of that artifact came out of the model over the wire.
- (`WORKFLOW_REFERENCE_STEPS.md` — the raw `lathe flow` dump — is the machine-truthful step index.)

*The hypothesis going in (owner's): the harness version should be better than the direct one. It is — and
this run earns it honestly, because the harness edition was produced by the real endpoint, not a
reproduction of its method.*

## How this run was actually done (so it can't be called a hijacked harness)
The `:8787` analyst endpoint was down earlier, and a prior attempt reproduced the harness *method* by hand.
That is no longer true. For this edition I brought the endpoint up the harness's own way — the shipped
`claude_proxy.py` wrapping the subscription `claude` CLI (opus) — and confirmed the full path end-to-end
(`request_spec('…') → 'HARNESS PATH LIVE'`). The document was then generated by three sequential analyst
calls over that path (draft 343 s, review 137 s, fold-back 161 s). The proxy runs `claude -p` with **tools
disabled** (pure text completion), so the analyst could only cite what was in its prompt — I fed it the
doctrine, `workflows.py`, `spine_core.py`, `run_gates.py`, the `run_spine` body, `GATES_REFERENCE.md`, and
the canonical step dump. **Then I independently verified its citations** — including three (`assumption_logic.py:49`,
`assumption_logic.py:84`, `persona_select.py:4`) for files it was *not* given — and every one checked out
exactly. So the harness edition is model-authored and *then* verified: trusted because checked, not because
it sounded right.

## Verdict first
**The through-the-harness edition is the better document.** It is more source-grounded, and — decisively —
its own review pass **caught real defects in its own draft** before shipping, on the exact axis the project
claims (verified > asserted). The direct edition, written in one confident pass, carries no such internal
check.

## Same in both
- Identical **21 workflows** (6 named + 15 per-invocation) and identical **step sequences** (both derive
  from `workflows.py`; they cannot disagree on what runs).
- Same **contracts** (when/entry/deliverable/done) for the six named workflows.
- Both explain the operating-contract spine, the thinking dial, and the AUTO/GATE/YOU typing.

## Where the harness edition won

| Axis | Direct (Fable) | Through the harness |
|---|---|---|
| **Grounding** | Prose from the source; few explicit `file:line` cites. | `file:line` on essentially every claim; independently re-verified true. |
| **Self-review** | None — single pass. | A real CE review pass over its own draft; **3 defects caught + 1 open question resolved** (below). |
| **Caught real defects in its own work** | No. | **Yes** — see the review payoff. |
| **Density** | ~195 lines, more narrative. | ~137 lines, every line load-bearing and cited. |

### The review payoff (what the harness loop caught in its OWN draft)
1. **Wrong citation range.** The draft cited the doctrine as `CLAUDE.md:9-19`; the review checked the file —
   the block is `9-17`, line 18 is blank and **line 19 is the next section's header**. Fixed to `9-17`,
   each sentence pinned to its own sub-line. *A self-caught over-cite — the failure mode the harness exists
   to stop.*
2. **Mis-grouped gate.** The draft lumped all three undocumented standing gates as "the '#12' spine/manifest
   gates." The review split them: `manifest_contract` and `spine_enforced` fit, but `gate_tristate`
   (`run_gates.py:33`) is a fail-closed/tristate guard — its own comment says so. Regrouped so a skeptic
   reading the source can't call it wrong.
3. **Overclaimed "runnable."** The draft called all 15 per-invocation workflows a "runnable table." The
   review found `onboard-project` is a `[YOU]`-only alias of `new-project` with no typeable command.
   Corrected to "14 of 15 runnable," with the exception named.
4. **Resolved an open question the direct pass never even raised.** `--think=high` stamps
   `LATHE_ASSUMPTION_POLICY=high+med` (`spine_core.py:17`) — a token outside the documented vocabulary. The
   harness edition traced it: policy dispatch is by **substring** (`elif 'med' in p:`, `assumption_logic.py:49`
   & `:84`), so `high+med` matches `med` and is a silently-accepted alias, not a bug. (I verified this
   against the file.)
5. **Carried the standing-gate drift forward, with the fix spelled out.** Both editions surface that
   `GATES_REFERENCE.md` documents 7 standing gates while `run_gates.py:24-33` runs 10; the harness edition
   goes further and specifies the three missing rows (manifest-contract, spine-enforced, fail-closed/tristate).

## Where the direct edition still holds an edge
Honesty cuts both ways:
- The direct edition carries a fuller **`CONTRACT_FOR` flag table**, a **capstone-caveats section** tying the
  workflows to the just-fixed v2.16→2.18 findings, and more per-workflow narrative — context the terse
  harness edition trims.
- On the *steps* both are equally correct (shared source), so for "just tell me the sequence" the `STEPS`
  dump beats both.

## The meta-finding (this is the point)
Same model tier, same source, two methods — and the **structured method produced the more trustworthy
artifact and caught defects in its own first draft that the free pass had no mechanism to catch.** That is
the value proposition, demonstrated on this very document: *discipline beats a confident first draft.* And
this time there is no method-reproduction caveat — the harness edition went through the real analyst
endpoint, and its citations were verified after the fact rather than taken on faith.
